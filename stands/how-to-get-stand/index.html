🎣 Arrow Shard Fishing Complete Guide

⚠️ Critical Information

  • ▸ Drop Rate: 0.05% chance per fish caught
  • ▸ Cannot be stored: Arrow Shards cannot be banked or stored
  • ▸ Cannot be dropped: No way to give to other players
  • ▸ Cannot be traded: Personal use only
  • ▸ Lost on death: Death or leaving server destroys Arrow Shard
  • ▸ Usage requirement: Must not have existing Stand to use

Step-by-Step Fishing Guide

1. 🚂 Find Tackle Bait Shop

Navigation Method:

  • • Follow the train tracks from any location
  • • Look for train stops along the route
  • • Tackle Bait shop is at the second train stop
  • • Alternative: Wait for train and ride it
  • • Use horse (press H) to travel faster along tracks

2. 🛒 Purchase Equipment

Required Items:

  • • Talk to the NPC inside the shop
  • • Select "Buy Gear" option
  • • Purchase a fishing rod
  • • Purchase fishing bait
  • • Ensure you have enough Moola for both

3. 🎣 Fishing Process

Fishing Mechanics:

  • • Find any water source (rivers, lakes, ponds)
  • • Equip fishing rod and approach water
  • • Watch for blue indicator near your character
  • • Click left mouse button when blue appears
  • • Repeat until Arrow Shard drops (0.05% chance)

🚨 Arrow Shard Safety Protocol

Immediate Actions Upon Getting Arrow Shard:

  1. Stop fishing immediately
  2. Check you have no existing Stand
  3. Use Arrow Shard right away
  4. Do not travel or engage in combat
  5. Stay in safe area until used

Why This Protocol Matters:

  • • Arrow Shards are extremely rare (0.05%)
  • • Death means losing hours of fishing work
  • • Cannot be recovered once lost
  • • No insurance or backup methods
  • • Must start fishing process over completely

🌊 Water Escape Tactics - "The Water Method"

🏆 Why Water Escape is the Meta Strategy

The "Water Method" is currently the most effective survival strategy for Corpse Part holders. It exploits game mechanics to make you nearly impossible to catch while building up your success probability.

Advantages:

  • • Speed: Move like a speedboat across water
  • • Unpredictability: Hard to track your movement
  • • Distance: Quickly get far from spawn point
  • • Stamina recovery: Can recover while moving
  • • Terrain advantage: Most players avoid water

Success Rate:

  • • Solo players: 40-60% success with water method
  • • vs Land escape: 5-15% success rate
  • • Time gained: 15-20+ seconds easily achievable
  • • Pursuit difficulty: 90% of chasers give up
  • • Consistency: Works in most server situations

Complete Water Method Execution

1. 🏃 Immediate Response

Upon Corpse Part Pickup:

  • • Don't panic: Stay calm and focused
  • • Press H immediately: Get on your horse
  • • Identify nearest water: Rivers, lakes, ponds
  • • Ignore other players: Don't engage in combat
  • • Head straight to water: Shortest route possible

2. 🌊 Water Entry & Movement

Water Navigation:

  • • Enter water on horseback: Don't dismount
  • • Constant movement: Never stop moving
  • • Speedboat style: Fast, erratic patterns
  • • Use full stamina: Maximum speed initially
  • • Change directions: Unpredictable movement

3. ⚡ Stamina Management

Recovery Technique:

  • • When stamina low: Press S key (backwards)
  • • Backward speed: ~20 speed while recovering
  • • Still moving: Maintain distance from pursuers
  • • Recovery time: 3-5 seconds usually enough
  • • Resume forward: Back to full speed movement

🎯 Advanced Water Tactics

Optimal Water Bodies:

  • • Large lakes: Most space for maneuvering
  • • Rivers: Good for linear escape routes
  • • Connected waterways: Multiple escape options
  • • Deep water: Harder for enemies to follow
  • • Avoid shallow areas: Slower movement speed

Psychological Warfare:

  • • Distance discouragement: Most players give up
  • • Time pressure: They know you're building probability
  • • Effort vs reward: Chasing becomes inefficient
  • • Multiple targets: Other Corpse Parts may spawn
  • • Stamina disadvantage: Chasers tire faster

🎯 Server Selection Strategy

🔍 Finding Low-Competition Servers

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Open server list: Click on server browser tab
  2. Sort by player count: Click "Descend" then "Ascend"
  3. Target 3-5 players: Minimum for spawns, low competition
  4. Check server stability: Avoid servers with fluctuating counts
  5. Join and monitor: Watch for Corpse Part announcements

Pro Tip: Bookmark good servers and return to them regularly.
