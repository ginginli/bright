Complete Bridger Western Beginner Guide

Start your journey in Ridge B Valley

Based on verified beginner guide videos | Updated: April 2026

🎮 Step 1: Character Creation

From Guide Video 2:

"So, the second that you're in the game, you're going to see create character, career, and tutorial, right? You want to press create character, obviously."

Character Creation Options:

  • Create Character - Start a new character
  • Career - View your progression
  • Tutorial - Game tutorial (optional)

⚖️ Alignment Selection

Outlaw vs Inlaw:

  • Outlaw (Recommended): More freedom, suggested by guide creators
  • Inlaw: More restrictive, currently not much difference in mechanics
"I suggest going outlaw, you have more freedom. It's pretty easy. Like, I'll tell you the best thing, just go outlaw." - Guide Video 2

📍 Spawn Point Selection

Always choose Red Corner:

  • Closest spawn point to fishing area
  • Essential for quick early-game progression
  • Reduces travel time to key locations
"And then you're going to want to spawn in Red Corner, okay? Just spawn in Red Corner, it's the closest thing to the fishing." - Guide Video 2

💰 Step 2: Initial Moola Strategy

Starting Money RNG:

When you first spawn in, you get random starting Moola:

  • Range: 50 to 500 Moola (random chance)
  • Target: At least 200-300 Moola for a good start
"As you can see, I'm at 377, so that's fine. If you don't have like above 200 at least, I'm going to show you what to do if you don't have at least 300 cuz when you first spawn in, you have a random chance of getting between like 50 to 500." - Guide Video 2

🔄 Wipe Strategy

If you get less than 200 Moola:

  1. Press Wipe button in character menu
  2. Create character again
  3. Select Outlaw alignment
  4. Spawn in Red Corner
  5. Check your new starting Moola
"If you don't get like at least 300, let me tell you guys what to do. Once you're over here, you're going to want to press wipe, okay, and then create character, do the same thing, go over to Red Corner, press play." - Guide Video 2

🎣 Step 3: Fishing Start

Why start with fishing?

  • Safest Moola source - No PvP risk
  • Consistent income from common fish
  • Potential for rare drops (Arrow Shard 0.05%, Golden Bass 15k Moola)
  • Levels you up - Fishing gives XP for tier progression
"Like that is where every single people in this game to get started because that is how you level up easily as well. I mean there's other way to level up, but you don't have to do that really. Uh if you want like a chill way of leveling up, you could do you could actually just do fishing, bro." - Guide Video 1

🐎 Step 4: Horse Upgrade

Getting a better horse:

  1. Go to Rich B. County (follow train from fishing area)
  2. Find horse NPC in front of train station
  3. Talk to NPC to see available horses

Horse selection strategy:

  • Only buy 1,000+ Moola horses
  • Cheaper horses have bad stats and are slow
  • 1,000-1,500 Moola horses have best stats

🚀 Horse Water Tech

Essential movement technique:

  1. Mount your horse (press N)
  2. Start sprinting (hold Shift)
  3. Enter water while sprinting
  4. Immediately hold Space bar
"Let me show you guys this horse tech, bro. Like hold shift and then once you go into the water, just hold space. Like yeah, you can actually travel the water with horse fast. Yeah, this is very useful tech, bro." - Guide Video 1

🃏 Step 5: Unlocking Cards

Finding the Mud Witch:

  1. From fishing area, use horse water tech to cross to swamp
  2. Look for hut in swamp area
  3. Enter hut and talk to Mud Witch

Card unlocking process:

  • First card: Free
  • Additional cards: 50 Moola each
  • Maximum: 3 cards per character
  • Cost: 150 Moola per roll (choose 1 from 3 options)

Quick Reference Checklist

Create character with Outlaw alignment
Spawn in Red Corner
Ensure 200+ Moola starting money (wipe if needed)
Go to fishing area (follow train tracks)
Buy fishing rod (150 Moola) and bait (15 Moola)
Fish until you have 1,000+ Moola
Buy better horse (1,000+ Moola cost)
Use horse water tech to reach swamp
Visit Mud Witch to unlock first free card
